Nsp Skill Reading Blood Pressure

Ski Patrol Skill for performing a Blood Pressure Reading.

Steps to Measure Blood Pressure:

  1. Prepare and Position:
  2. Ensure the scene is safe and take standard precautions.
  3. Introduce yourself and obtain permission to help.

  4. Select the correct cuff size (regular, large adult, or pediatric) to ensure accurate readings.

  5. Apply the Cuff:

  6. Deflate the cuff completely and place it on the patient’s upper arm, 1–2 inches above the elbow crease.
  7. Ensure the arrow on the cuff points to the brachial artery, located on the inner side of the arm at the elbow.

  8. Place the cuff directly on the skin, not over clothing, and ensure it is snug but not tight.

  9. Position the Equipment:

  10. Place the sphygmomanometer where you can easily read it without holding it.

  11. Insert the stethoscope ear tips into your ears, facing forward.

  12. Locate the Brachial Pulse:

  13. Palpate the brachial artery at the elbow crease and place the stethoscope diaphragm over the pulse point. Hold it lightly against the skin.

  14. Inflate the Cuff:

  15. Tighten the valve and inflate the cuff using the bulb.
  16. Inflate to 160 mmHg. If you hear a pulse, increase to 200 mmHg. If the pulse is still audible, continue in 20 mmHg increments until you can no longer hear it.

Systolic and Diastolic Blood Pressure:

  • Systolic Blood Pressure: This is the top number in a blood pressure reading. It measures the pressure in the arteries when the heart contracts and pumps blood out to the body. It indicates how much pressure the blood exerts against the artery walls when the heart is actively beating.

  • Diastolic Blood Pressure: This is the bottom number in a blood pressure reading. It measures the pressure in the arteries when the heart is at rest between beats. This shows how much pressure the blood exerts on the artery walls when the heart is filling with blood.

Normal Blood Pressure Readings:

  • Normal Blood Pressure: A typical reading is around 120/80 mmHg.
  • Systolic: 90–120 mmHg
  • Diastolic: 60–80 mmHg
  • Elevated Blood Pressure: 120–129 systolic and less than 80 diastolic.
  • Hypertension (High Blood Pressure):
  • Stage 1: 130–139 systolic or 80–89 diastolic.
  • Stage 2: 140 or higher systolic or 90 or higher diastolic.
  • Hypotension (Low Blood Pressure): Generally, readings below 90/60 mmHg are considered low and may indicate hypotension.
